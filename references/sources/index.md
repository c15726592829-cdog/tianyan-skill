# Tianyan Source Rule Library

This directory separates received classical text, Han-to-Song structural Yi
material, and later Liuyao operational manuals. Source IDs are evidence hooks
for question-type modules; they are not proof that divination is predictive.

## Verification Status

`A` means checked against a reliable scan or authoritative critical edition.
`B` means confirmed by two independent classical transcriptions or sources.
`C` means unproofread electronic text used only for navigation; it cannot govern
a strong conclusion.

Question modules may cite `A` or `B` source records as governing rules. `C`
records are allowed in the source library but must not be used as governing
module rules.

## Conflict Policy

When source layers conflict, the narrower operational rule governs only inside
its declared conditions. Received Zhouyi and Shuo Gua images provide symbolic
corroboration; they do not silently replace Najia, Shi/Ying, useful-spirit,
month/day, or moving-line judgment. Later manuals may operationalize divination
practice, but their rules must not be presented as literal Zhouyi wording.

## Copying Policy

Records quote only short source fragments or traditional formulas needed for
identification. Modern commentary is paraphrased as operational rules and
modern extensions, not copied as authority. If exact wording has not been
verified, the record must be marked `C` and cannot appear in a module's
governing Source Rule IDs.
