# Classical Interpretation Rules for Wenwang Liuyao

This reference defines a restrained, reproducible interpretation layer. It does
not claim that traditional divination is scientifically predictive. It exists
to prevent free association, hindsight fitting, and rule changes after an
answer is known.

## Source Layers

Keep the historical layers distinct:

1. The received `周易` supplies hexagram and line texts.
2. `说卦传` supplies canonical trigram images and body correspondences, such as
   `坎为耳` and `艮为手`.
3. The Jing Fang / Najia lineage supplies palace, stem-branch, relative, and
   calendrical structures.
4. Later operational manuals, including `火珠林`, `增删卜易`, and
   `卜筮正宗`, systematize useful-spirit, month/day, movement, void, break,
   combination, clash, hidden spirit, and timing practice.

Do not present a later manual rule as the literal wording of the Zhouyi. When
manuals differ, disclose the convention used instead of silently combining the
most favorable pieces.

## Evidence Labels

Every material conclusion must distinguish these layers:

- **Chart fact**: deterministic script output or directly supplied data.
- **Classical rule**: a named correspondence or operational rule.
- **Inference**: application of the rule to this question.
- **Contrary evidence**: a chart factor or real fact that weakens the inference.
- **Speculation**: an unverified candidate, analogy, or named-entity guess.

Do not move a claim upward after the truth is revealed. A speculation remains a
speculation even when it happens to match.

## Perspective Before Useful Spirit

First define the grammatical and real-world roles:

- Who is asking?
- Who or what is the subject?
- Is the question about identity, state, action, destination, companion,
  timing, result, or advice?
- Which side is self, which is the named counterpart, and which is the matter?

Only then select the useful spirit. The hierarchy is:

```text
question and perspective
-> person or matter anchor
-> useful spirit
-> month and day
-> moving and changed lines
-> Shi/Ying relations
-> branch structures
-> six spirits
-> hexagram/trigram corroboration
-> real-world constraints
-> bounded conclusion
```

Six relatives describe relations to the palace element and roles in the
question. 妻财 and 官鬼 are **not universal gender labels**. Never infer
`woman`, `man`, `wife`, `husband`, or `boyfriend` from those names alone.

## Shi/Ying and Third Parties

Shi normally anchors the questioner's side. Ying normally anchors the external
counterpart, target side, or conditions outside the questioner's control.

The operational rule for a named third party is: when the subject is a
**specific friend or outside person**, normally anchor that person at Ying,
consistent with the later manual formula `占朋友、外人以应爻为用神`. Then use
the matter-specific relative as secondary evidence when the question asks about
that person's money, documents, travel, partner, illness, or another concrete
matter.

Do not switch between Ying, 兄弟, 妻财, or 官鬼 merely because another anchor
produces a better retrospective answer. State the anchor before interpretation.

## Movement Is Not Existence

Movement means activity, change, activation, or an operative relation. It does
not prove success, identity, physical presence, or existence.

Conversely, a **static line does not mean absence**. A static line may represent
a present person or condition; its ability to manifest depends on relevance,
strength, support, void/break, concealment, and activation by month/day or a
moving line.

Use these separate questions:

1. Is this the correct reference line?
2. Is the referent represented or present?
3. Is the line active now?
4. Can its action manifest?

Do not answer all four from the moving/static flag alone.

## Month/Day/Branch Structures

Judge month and day before branch storytelling. Month is seasonal authority;
day can support, control, combine, clash, fill void, or activate. Movement and
changed branches are then judged only where they touch the selected anchor or
useful spirit.

A six-combination, six-clash, or **three-combination** is first a branch
relationship. It is not by itself a person, journey, city, event, or completed
five-element transformation. Require the actual branches, sufficient strength,
and relevance to the selected reference.

Six spirits are secondary imagery. They cannot override useful-spirit choice,
month/day strength, motion, or explicit reality.

## Location and Direction

Use location cues in this order:

1. Select the correct person or travel reference.
2. Determine inner/outer placement and Shi/Ying perspective.
3. Read relevant trigram direction and image.
4. Read the selected branch's direction/element as corroboration.
5. Check movement, changed direction, combination, clash, void, and break.
6. Compare with known travel constraints or a predeclared candidate set.

Do not treat a branch combination as a literal map. An open-world prediction of
a **named modern city** is speculative unless at least two independent chart
signals converge and the candidate set or geographic frame was fixed before
the answer. Without that, report only a broad direction, environment, or travel
quality, and label city names as low-confidence alternatives.

## Timing

Timing starts from the selected reference and a stated activation condition:

- leaving or filling void
- recovery from month break
- a relevant moving line meeting, combining, or clashing
- changed branch becoming effective
- seasonal support arriving
- a strong static line being activated by a justified day/month relation

A **branch match alone** cannot justify an exact date. State the causal rule,
calendar unit, timezone, and competing rules. If several windows fit or the
hexagram is static without a clear trigger, give a range or relative speed and
lower confidence. Never manufacture a precise departure date from one branch.

## Object Identification

For a held or hidden object, begin with direct canonical images before narrative
associations. Examples from `说卦传` include `坎为耳`, `离为目`, `艮为手`, and
`兑为口`. Body correspondences describe image domains; they do not uniquely
name a modern product.

An exact object answer requires at least two independent signals, for example:

- a direct trigram/body correspondence tied to the question wording
- a relevant line or changed trigram supporting the same object class
- element/material/function evidence that is not derived from the first signal
- a fixed candidate set supplied before the cast

Prefer category before exact item. `坎为耳` can support an ear-related object,
but a specific audio device requires independent corroboration; it must not be
reverse-fit after disclosure.

## Confidence and Resolution

Record both confidence and answer resolution:

- **Strong**: selected anchor, month/day, movement, corroborating image, and
  known facts align with no major contradiction.
- **Medium**: the direction is coherent but one important layer is mixed.
- **Weak**: anchor is ambiguous, evidence conflicts, or the answer is an exact
  modern identity, city, companion, or date without independent support.

Apply these hard resolution caps even when a forced guess is requested:

- **open-world named modern city: weak**; keep it in speculation unless the
  candidate set was fixed before the cast
- **exact date or clock time: weak**; a justified window may be medium, but an
  exact point is not raised by branch matching
- **exact modern object: at most medium**, and only with two genuinely
  independent signals; otherwise weak
- exact companion or relationship identity: weak unless the candidate set and
  identity rule were fixed before reveal

Resolution levels are: broad class, bounded category, ranked candidate, exact
named entity, and exact time. Do not use strong language at a finer resolution
than the evidence supports. Give contrary evidence beside the conclusion.

## Common Failures

- Rewriting the user's question so it contains the revealed answer.
- Choosing the useful spirit after reading the ground truth.
- Treating six-relative names as literal gender or relationship labels.
- Treating static as absent or moving as certainly present/successful.
- Turning a three-combination into a geographic name.
- Selecting a city from an unrestricted national map.
- Selecting an exact date because one branch matches.
- Listing many alternatives and later claiming whichever one matched.
- Counting a post-reveal reinterpretation as a pre-reveal success.
- Omitting contrary evidence or silently raising confidence after feedback.
